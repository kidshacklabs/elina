How Can We Help?




Ways we can Help

Here are some ways we can help save our bees along with other pollenators!

  • Plant! If you plant more flowers, there will be more pollen for bees to spread!
  • Bees get thirsty! Leave out a small basin of water for them to drink out of.
  • Don't use fertilizers! Fertilizers are harmful to the bees and other pollenators!
  • Support! Support local beekeepers and bee protection organizations!
  • Buy local! Buy raw honey from your local beekeepers!
  • Don't get rid of dandelions or other weeds! They are a great source of food for the bees. Plus, it helps more native species such as wild strawberries, trout lilies, and blue violets grow!
  • Educate! Educating others about the important of bees is important! Most people view bees as scary monsters, when in reality they're harmless!
  • Sign! Sign petitions to help protect the bees on websites such as change.org.
  • Join! Join the PollinateTO program, a program which offers $5000 to the people who create the best pollenator-friendly gardens!
  • Speak up! Do what Sandy did! Take a trip to your city hall and demand a change!
  • Each season! Make sure at least one plant in your garden is blooming every season!
  • Mulch-free! Don't use mulch!
  • Get Messy! Gardens that are too tidy aren't friendly to wildlife, so leave some messy patches that bees and other animals will use to construct nests!
  • Check! Make sure that you are buying organic seeds! You wouldn't want to accidentally use insecticides like Sandy was!


Fun Facts About Bees!

  • Bees have five eyes!
  • Bees have two stomachs, one is used for their food and
  • A queen bee lives for 1-5 years while a worker bee lives for 30 days.
  • Bees rarely sting people and animals, because if they do then they die.
  • Bees are vegetarians unlike wasps, which are carnivores.
  • About one third of what we eat relies on the pollenation of bees (things like fruits, vegetables, ant nuts)
